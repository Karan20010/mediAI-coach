Insoluble calcium (eg, calcium carbonate) requires an acidic environment for proper

absorption. Strong acid-suppressing medications, such as proton pump inhibitors (eg,

omeprazole), may decrease absorption of dietary calcium in the gastrointestinal tract.

Long-term use of these medications is associated with an increased risk of osteoporotic

fractures. The risk is greatest in older adults in whom calcium absorption is already reduced Activate Windows
