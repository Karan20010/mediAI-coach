Educational objective:

Hematogenous osteomyelitis is predominantly a disease of children that most frequently
affects the long bones. Staphylococcus aureus is implicated in most cases secondary to a
bacteremic event. Streptococcus pyogenes (group A streptococcus) is the second most
common cause of hematogenous osteomyelitis.
