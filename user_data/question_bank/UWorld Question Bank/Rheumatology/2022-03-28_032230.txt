Educational objective:

Ankylosing spondylitis is characterized by stiffness and fusion of axial joints (ankylosis) and
inflammation at the site of insertion of tendons into bone (enthesitis). Involvement of the
thoracic spine and costovertebral and costosternal junctions can limit chest wall expansion,

leading to hypoventilation.
