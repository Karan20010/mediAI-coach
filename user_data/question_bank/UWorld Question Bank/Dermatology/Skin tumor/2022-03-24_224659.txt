(Choice A) The endoderm gives rise to all structures derived from the inner lining of the
primitive gut tube. These include the thyroid follicular cells; epithelial surfaces of the trachea,
bronchi, and lungs; liver and biliary tree; pancreas; and gastrointestinal and bladder
epithelium.

(Choice B) The mesoderm gives rise to the dermis as well as most bones, muscles, blood
vessels, and visceral tissue.

(Choice D) The neuroectoderm gives rise to the central nervous system, preganglionic
autonomic neurons, retina, and posterior pituitary.

(Choice E) The surface ectoderm gives rise to the epidermis and its appendages, mammary
glands (modified sweat glands), lens of the eye, and adenohypophysis.
