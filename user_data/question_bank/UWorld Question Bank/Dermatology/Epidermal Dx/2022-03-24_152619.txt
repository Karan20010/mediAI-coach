(Choice A) An androgen-induced increase in sebum production causes acne vulgaris, which
presents with comedones, inflammatory papules, pustules, and nodules during adolescence.
Diffuse scaling is not characteristic.

(Choice B) Autoantibodies against desmogleins 1 and 3 cause pemphigus vulgaris, which is
characterized by flaccid bullae that easily rupture to form erosions. Scaling is not
characteristic.

(Choice D) Excessive collagen deposition is seen in systemic sclerosis (ie, scleroderma).
Systemic sclerosis presents with diffuse thickening and hardening of the skin, often associated
with ulcerations at the fingertips.

(Choice E) Allergic contact dermatitis is a type IV (T-cell-mediated) hypersensitivity reaction
triggered by a variety of allergens (eg, poison ivy, natural rubber). It typically presents acutely
within 2-4 days of exposure with a localized, pruritic, erythematous rash with vesicles and
small bullae.
