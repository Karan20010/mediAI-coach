This patient is experiencing the classic symptoms of opioid withdrawal. Opioid withdrawal
can start as soon as 6-12 hours after cessation of a short-acting opioid such as heroin and
usually peaks within 24-48 hours. Symptoms include nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal
cramping, and myalgia. It is generally nonlife-threatening and vital signs are usually normal.
Dilated pupils, prominent piloerection, and hyperactive bowel sounds are seen on
examination. Opioid withdrawal can appear similar to other withdrawal or intoxication
syndromes, but certain findings, including lacrimation and yawning, are fairly specific.

Individuals who have been taking chronic prescription opioids, such as cancer patients, can
also develop opioid withdrawal on abrupt discontinuation or marked reduction in usage.
