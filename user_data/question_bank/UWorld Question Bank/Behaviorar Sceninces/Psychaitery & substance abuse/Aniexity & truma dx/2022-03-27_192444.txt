(Choice E) Quetiapine is a second-generation antipsychotic that is associated with numerous

potentially severe side effects, including sedation, weight gain, and metabolic syndrome. It is
not recommend for treating insomnia in patients without psychosis.

Educational objective:

When benzodiazepines are used in the treatment of anxiety, drug selection should consider
the medication's duration of action. Short- to intermediate-acting benzodiazepines (eg,
lorazepam) are preferred in situations in which prolonged side effects of sedation and cognitive
impairment must be minimized.
