Major depressive episode & grief reaction

Major depressive episode

Grief reaction

22 weeks; 25 of 9 symptoms: low mood,
anhedonia, sleep disturbance, appetite
change, low energy, psychomotor
changes, guilt/worthlessness,
concentration difficulty, suicidal ideation
May occur in response to a variety of
stressors, including loss of a loved one
Marked social & occupational dysfunction
Suicidality related to hopelessness &
worthlessness

Normal reaction to loss (bereavement)
Sadness more specific to thoughts of the
deceased

"Waves" of grief at reminders
Self-esteem usually preserved
Functional decline less severe

Thoughts of dying involve wish to join the
deceased; active suicidality uncommon
Intensity decreases over time
