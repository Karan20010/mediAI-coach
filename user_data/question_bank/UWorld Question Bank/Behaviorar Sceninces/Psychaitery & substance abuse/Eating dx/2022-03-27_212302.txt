Educational objective:

Hypokalemia in an otherwise healthy young adult with a normal BMI and preoccupation with
body size is concerning for self-induced vomiting associated with bulimia nervosa. Common
physical examination findings in bulimia nervosa include tachycardia, hypotension, painless
bilateral parotid gland swelling, calluses or scarring on the dorsum of the hand, and erosion of
dental enamel.
