(Choice D) Red blood cell sickling occurs in sickle cell anemia, which is caused by anA to T
mutation in the B globin gene that results in glutamate being substituted with valine at position
6.

Educational objective:
6 thalassemia results in hypochromic, microcytic anemia due to decreased B globin chain

synthesis. Unpaired a chains precipitate within red cells and cause membrane damage,
leading to ineffective erythropoiesis and hemolysis.
