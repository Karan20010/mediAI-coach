Educational objective:

The Beers criteria identify drugs that should be used with caution in geriatric patients.
Common drugs to avoid include anticholinergics (eg, first-generation antihistamines), centrally
acting alpha-2 agonists, tricyclic antidepressants, benzodiazepines (and other sedating
medications), antipsychotics, many antiarrhythmics (eg, digoxin), and skeletal muscle
relaxants.
