(Choice C) The correlation coefficient is a measure of the strength and direction of a linear
relationship between 2 quantitative variables. For example, a study may report a correlation

(Choice D) Meta-analysis is an epidemiological method of pooling the data from several
studies to conduct an analysis having a relatively larger statistical power than that of the
individual studies.

Educational objective:

A 2 x 2 table is normally used to record the presence or absence of exposure and disease in
research. Rows and columns represent the different levels for each categorical (ie, exposure
and disease) variable. The chi-square test for independence is used to evaluate the
association between 2 categorical variables.
