Tinea pedis

Etiology

¢ Trichophyton species
(most common)

Clinical
features

¢ Pruritus, erythema,
erosions/scaling

¢ Most often interdigital or
covering the sole
(moccasin type)

¢ Segmented hyphae on
KOH preparation

Treatment

¢ Topical antifungal therapy
(eg, azoles, terbinafine)

¢ Keep feet dry & dispose
of old footwear
