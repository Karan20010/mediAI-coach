Nonbullous impetigo

Microbiology

¢ Staphylococcus aureus (most common)

¢ Group A Streptococcus (S pyogenes)

Clinical features

Papules & pustules with honey-crusted lesions
May be painful or itchy
Most commonly involves face or extremities in young children

Treatment

Topical antibiotics (eg, mupirocin) if localized
Oral antibiotics (eg, cephalexin) if extensive

Complications

Poststreptococcal glomerulonephritis
