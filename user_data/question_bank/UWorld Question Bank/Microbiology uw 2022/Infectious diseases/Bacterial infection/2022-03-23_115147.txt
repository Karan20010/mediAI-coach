Vibrio vulnificus

¢ Curved, gram-negative, free-living in salt water

; High concentrations in warm months
Epidemiology

Ingestion (raw oysters) or wound infection

+ Risk in those with liver disease & iron overload*

¢ Rapidly progressive (often <12 hours)
Manifestations |* Septicemia — Septic shock, bullous lesions

Cellulitis — Hemorrhagic bullae, necrotizing fasciitis

*Hereditary hemochromatosis is particularly high risk as iron acts as a growth catalyst.
