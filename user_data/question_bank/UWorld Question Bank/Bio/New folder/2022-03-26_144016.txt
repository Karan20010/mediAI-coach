Educational Objective:

Exertional dyspnea, pneumonia resulting in life-threatening acute chest syndrome, and
recurrent abdominal and bone pain are clinical features of sickle cell anemia. Sickle cell
anemia results from a point mutation that causes valine to substitute for glutamic acid in the
sixth position of the b-globin chain of hemoglobin.
