(Choice A) Vibrio cholerae is an oxidase-positive, Gram-negative, comma-shaped rod that
can grow on an alkaline medium that kills most organisms. V cholerae causes a toxin-
mediated (stimulates adenylyl cyclase) watery diarrhea.

(Choice B) Gram-negative rods that are fast lactose fermenters include Klebsiella and
Escherichia coli. They commonly cause urinary tract infections.

(Choice D) Shigella is a Gram-negative, nonlactose-fermenting, nonmotile, rod-shaped
bacterium. It causes shigellosis, a hemorrhagic diarrheal disease.

(Choice E) Haemophilus influenza is Gram-negative, rod-shaped bacterium that requires
factor X (hematin) and factor V (NAD+) for growth. Nontypable (unencapsulated) H influenzae
is a frequent cause of acute otitis media in children and adults. Acute otitis media is
associated with an inflamed, erythematous, bulging, immobile tympanic membrane due to the
presence of infected fluid in the middle ear.
