Classic findings include short neck, low-set ears, epicanthic folds, and slanted palpebral

Educational objective:

Fragile X syndrome, an X-linked disorder, is the most common cause of inherited intellectual
disability. The pathogenesis involves an unstable expansion of trinucleotide repeats (CGG) in
the fragile X mental retardation 1 (FMR71) gene. Key physical findings include dysmorphic
facial features (eg, large jaw, protruding ears, long face) and macroorchidism.
