(Choice E) Pleiotropy refers to the phenomenon in which a single gene influences multiple
phenotypic traits.

Educational objective:

Genomic imprinting refers to the phenomenon in which an offspring's genes are expressed in
a parent-specific manner. Genomic imprinting is caused by DNA methylation, an epigenetic
process in which genes can be silenced by attaching methyl groups to cytosine residues in the
DNA molecule.
