(Choice A) Anal sexual intercourse is more likely to transmit pathogens such as HIV,
Neisseria gonorrhoeae, and Chlamydia trachomatis.

(Choices C, D, and E) Urine can harbor pathogens such as cytomegalovirus, adenovirus,
Escherichia coli, Staphylococcus saprophyticus, and Klebsiella pneumoniae; blood can harbor
hepatitis B virus, hepatitis C virus, and HIV; and arthropods (eg, ticks) can harbor Rickettsia
rickettsii and Borrelia burgdorferi, among others.

(Choice F) Malaria and leishmaniasis, which can cause splenomegaly, are found in South
Asia. However, malaria would classically be associated with anemia and thrombocytopenia.
Visceral leishmaniasis (kala-azar) manifests as progressive splenic enlargement and weight
loss over months.
