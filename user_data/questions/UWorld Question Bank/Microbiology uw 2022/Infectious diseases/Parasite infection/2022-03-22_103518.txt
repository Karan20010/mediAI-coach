Babesiosis

Epidemiology

Agent: Babesia microti

Vector: Ixodes scapularis tick (same
for Lyme disease & HGA)
Geographic distribution: northeastern
United States

Clinical
manifestations

Signs & symptoms: fevers, fatigue,
myalgias, headache, flulike
symptoms (if severe: ARDS, CHF,
DIC, splenic rupture)

Laboratory studies: anemia,
thrombocytopenia, t LFTs

Diagnosis

2.

Thin blood smear (Wright/Giemsa
stains): intraerythrocytic pleomorphic
ring forms (occasional "Maltese
crosses")

ARDS = acute respiratory distress syndrome; CHF = congestive

heart failure; DIC = disseminated intravascular coagulation; HGA

= human granulocytic anaplasmosis; LFTs = liver function tests.
