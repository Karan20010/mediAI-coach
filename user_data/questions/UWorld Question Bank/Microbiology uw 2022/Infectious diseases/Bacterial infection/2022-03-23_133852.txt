(Choice A) Epidemic fungal meningitis due to Aspergillus fumigatus has been associated with
contaminated glucocorticoid injections. Aspergillus is not typically associated with foodborne
illness.

(Choices B and D) Cytomegalovirus and herpes simplex virus can cause a variety of clinical
syndromes in immunocompromised patients, including encephalitis, esophagitis, pneumonitis,
and colitis. These viruses are ubiquitous and not associated with foodborne outbreaks.

(Choice C) Foodborne outbreaks of Escherichia coli 0157:H7 have been linked to the
ingestion of contaminated beef (primarily) and are usually associated with hemorrhagic colitis.
Patients may also develop hemolytic uremic syndrome. Meningitis and neonatal infection
would be atypical.

(Choice F) Neisseria meningitidis causes sepsis and acute meningitis. It is transmitted
primarily by respiratory droplets; most cases occur in those who live in congregate settings
(eg, dormitories, military barracks).

(Choice G) Streptococcus pneumoniae is a common cause of bacterial meningitis in elderly
and immunocompromised individuals but is not associated with foodborne outbreaks.
