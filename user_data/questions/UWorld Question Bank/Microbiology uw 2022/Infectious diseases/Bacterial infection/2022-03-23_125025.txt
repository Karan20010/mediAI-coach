Catheter-associated urinary tract infection

Clinical
features

e Fever, flank pain, suprapubic pain

¢ 210% cfu/mL of uropathogenic organism (eg, Escherichia coli,
Enterococcus, Klebsiella)

¢ Current (or within 48 hr) use of urethral, suprapubic, or condom
catheter

Risk factors

Duration of catheter use, especially >48 hr (most important)
e Female sex

e Advanced age

Diabetes mellitus

Prevention

Avoid unnecessary catheterization (eg, not indicated for
incontinence)
e Use sterile technique when placing catheter

Remove catheter promptly when no longer needed

cfu = colony-forming units.
