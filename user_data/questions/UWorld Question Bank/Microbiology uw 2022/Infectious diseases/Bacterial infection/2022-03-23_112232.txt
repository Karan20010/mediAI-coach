(Choice A) Trimethoprim and sulfamethoxazole are folate metabolism antagonists. They are
the first-line therapy for Pneumocystis pneumonia, which often presents with subacute
pulmonary symptoms and a bilateral patchy interstitial infiltrate. However, most patients have
significant shortness of breath and nearly all are immunocompromised (eg, AIDS, chronic
steroids).

(Choice D) Azole medications (eg, fluconazole) inhibit the synthesis of ergosterol, a primary
component of the fungal cell membrane. Fungal pneumonias are often associated with lobar,
nodular, or cavitary infiltrates. However, most occur in the setting of immunosuppression, and
an accompanying sore throat would be unusual.

(Choice E) Mycolic acid is found in the cell wall of Mycobacterium and can be targeted by
isoniazid, a mycolic acid synthesis inhibitor. Active pulmonary tuberculosis often presents with
several weeks of cough, weight loss, malaise, and upper lobe cavitary infiltrates.
