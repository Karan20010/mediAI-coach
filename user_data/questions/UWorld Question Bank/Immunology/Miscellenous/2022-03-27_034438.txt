Educational objective:

Morbilliform drug eruption is a type IV hypersensitivity reaction caused by drugs (eg,
anticonvulsants, antibiotics) or their metabolites that typically occurs 5-21 days following drug
initiation. Lesions consist of erythematous macules and papules, distributed symmetrically on
the trunk and extremities, sometimes accompanied by low-grade fever. Mucosal involvement
is absent.
