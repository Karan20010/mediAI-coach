(Choice G) Crystal arthritis due to calcium pyrophosphate deposition (pseudogout) most

Educational objective:

Gout results from the deposition of monosodium urate crystals in the joints and soft tissues.
Under polarized light, urate crystals appear needle-shaped and negatively birefringent.
Conditions that increase uric acid production or decrease uric acid clearance can increase the
risk of gout.
