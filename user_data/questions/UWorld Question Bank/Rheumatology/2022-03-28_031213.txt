(Choice C) Septic arthritis causes intense pain, swelling, and redness of affected joints. The
knee, hip, and shoulder are most commonly involved. The finger joints are less often affected
and bony joint enlargement is not typical. Moreover, systemic signs, including fever, chills, and
malaise, may be present.

(Choice E) Reactive arthritis is characterized by urethritis, conjunctivitis/uveitis, and arthritis
(mainly of the knees, ankles, and feet) and is most common in young men. Hand joint
involvement is not typical.

Educational objective:

Osteoarthritis of the hands is characterized by osteophyte formation leading to hard bony
enlargement of the distal interphalangeal joints (Heberden nodes) and proximal
interphalangeal joints (Bouchard nodes). Brief morning stiffness may be present.
