Acute onset of pain and swelling of the first metatarsophalangeal joint and joint aspiration
showing needle-shaped, negatively birefringent crystals are diagnostic for acute gouty
arthritis. Nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) are the mainstay of treatment for

most patients. However, colchicine is often used in patients with mild-to-moderate renal
failure, peptic ulcer disease, or other contraindications to NSAIDs. Colchicine is an effective
anti-inflammatory agent in acute gouty arthritis and acts by binding to the intracellular protein
tubulin, preventing tubulin polymerization into microtubules. This leads to impaired leukocyte
