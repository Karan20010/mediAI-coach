(Choices B, C, D, E, F, and G) These other agents are full agonists of the opioid mu-
receptor. Although they vary in potency, none have significant opioid receptor antagonist
properties. Use of these drugs in equianalgesic doses should not cause withdrawal.
Educational objective:

Buprenorphine is a partial opioid receptor agonist that binds with high affinity but has low
intrinsic activity. In patients on long-term opioid therapy, buprenorphine can displace other
opioids and precipitate withdrawal.
