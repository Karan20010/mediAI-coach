(Choice D) Delusional disorder is characterized by 21 delusions in the absence of other
psychotic symptoms. Functioning may be normal apart from the direct impact of the delusion.
Mood symptoms, if present, are not prominent.

(Choice E) In schizophreniform disorder, symptoms of schizophrenia (delusions,
hallucinations, disorganized speech and behavior, negative symptoms) are present for >1
month but <6 months. This patient's psychotic symptoms have occurred only in the context of
a manic episode and have been present for <1 month.

(Choice F) Substance-induced disorders should be ruled out prior to diagnosing primary
psychiatric illness. The extent of this patient's alcohol use would be unlikely to cause his
current symptoms. Cocaine and stimulants are more likely to induce manic symptoms.

Educational objective:

Bipolar | disorder is diagnosed in patients with 21 episodes of mania. Manic episodes are
characterized by elevated/irritable mood, impulsivity, hyperactivity, decreased need for sleep,
pressured speech, and grandiosity and may occur with psychotic features.
