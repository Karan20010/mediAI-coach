The data is presented in the standard 2 x 2 (contingency) table format. The sensitivity of a
test refers to its ability to correctly identify individuals affected with a disease. It is the number
of true positives divided by the total number of patients with the disease. Sensitivity should be

high in screening tests in order to pick up all cases of a disease (decrease false negatives).
