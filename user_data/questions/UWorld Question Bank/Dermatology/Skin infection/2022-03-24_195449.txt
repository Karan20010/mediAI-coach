(Choice D) Necrotic keratinocytes in the epidermis suggest Stevens-Johnson syndrome, a
severe reaction that occurs 1-3 weeks after an infection (eg, cytomegalovirus, Mycoplasma) or
drug exposure. Patients usually develop high fever, skin pain, mucosal erosions, and skin
detachment. However, it typically does not involve the palms and soles. In addition, this
patient has no evidence of skin detachment.
